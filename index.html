What is the mental health?

Mental health includes our emotional,psychological, and social well-being. It affects how we think,feel and act. It also helps determine how we handle stress, relate to others and make choices. Mental health is important at every stage of life, from childhood and adolescence through adulthood.
Many factors contribute to mental health problems,including:

  • Biological factors,such as genes or brain chemistry
  • Life experiences, such as trauma or abuse
  • Family history of mental health problems

Early Warning Signs

  1. Eating or sleeping too much or too little
  2. Pulling away from people and usual activities
  3. Having low or no energy
  4. Feeling numb or like nothing matters
  5. Having unexplained aches and pains
  6. Feeling helpless or hopeless
  7. feeling unusually confused,forgetful,on edge,angry,upset,worried or scared
  8. Thinking of harming yourself or others
  9. Inability to perform daily tasks
  10. Hearing voices or believing things that are not true
  11. Experiencing severe mood swings that cause problems in relationships

How to talk about mental health?

  • I have been worried about you. Can we talk about what are experiencing?
  • I am someone who cares and wants to listen .What do you want me to know about how you are feeling?
  • Who or What has helped deal with similar issues in the past?
  • Sometimes talking to someone who has dealt with a similar experience helps. Do you know of others who have experienced these types of problems who you can talk?
  • It seems like you are going through a difficult time. How can I help you to find help?
